Goita ごいた Rules

Goita is a traditional Japanese game from Noto, Ishikawa played with 32 tiles similar to Shogi pieces.

Setup

Four players split into two teams, sitting across from each other. Each player is dealt 8 tiles. Play proceeds counter-clockwise.

Objective

Be the first team to have a player play out all their tiles. First team to reach 150 points wins the game.

Gameplay

The dealer plays two tiles: one face down (defense) and one face up (attack). The next player can either:

  • Pass - Skip their turn
  • Play - Play two tiles: a defense tile that matches the previous attack, and a new attack tile

Matching Rules

  • Your defense tile must match the previous player's attack tile
  • Kings are wild and can match any tile except Lances and Pawns
  • Kings cannot be played as attack tiles unless one has already been played, or you have both Kings

Three Passes

If three players pass in a row, the original attacker can play any defense tile face down and choose a new attack.

Scoring

When a player plays their last tile, their team scores points based on the final attack tile's value. The winning player becomes the next dealer.

Winning

The first team to reach 150 points wins the game.
